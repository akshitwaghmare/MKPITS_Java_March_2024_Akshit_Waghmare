Traffic Penalties

Latest Traffic Penalties in India The Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 2019, introduced several changes to the traffic penalties in India. These changes were implemented to ensure stricter enforcement of traffic regulations and to reduce the number of road accidents in the country. Some of the latest traffic penalties in India include: Overspeeding: The penalty for overspeeding has been increased from Rs. 400 to Rs. 1,000 for light motor vehicles and from Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 2,000 for medium and heavy motor vehicles. Drunk driving: The penalty for drunk driving has been increased from Rs. 2,000 to Rs. 10,000 and can also lead to imprisonment for up to 6 months. Not wearing a helmet or seatbelt: The penalty for not wearing a helmet or seatbelt has been increased from Rs. 100 to Rs. 1,000. Using a mobile phone while driving: The penalty for using a mobile phone while driving has been increased from Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 5,000.

  • Overspeeding: Driving above the speed limit is a common offence that can result in hefty fines and penalties.
  • No entry sign: Indicates that the driver is not allowed to enter a particular road or area.
  • Speed limit sign: Indicates the maximum speed limit allowed on a particular road.
  • Traffic light signals: Used to control the flow of traffic at intersections and junctions.
